Daniel Lottis

Principal Engineer, Western Digital and IEEE Sight Chair

Daniel Lottis is a physicist with over twenty years of experience working with Spintronics and its precursors. Active in the Magnetism and Magnetic Materials field since 1987. He had early collaborations with partners in the Magnetic Recording industry involving perpendicular media and, later, GMR materials for sensors. He is recently active in the development of Spin-Transfer-Torque MRAM. Supporting collaborations between WD and academic research groups. Devoted volunteer in the Engineering for Development / Humanitarian Technology arena. Currently, he is a part of Western Digital's CTO Office team, where I coordinate our External Collaborations Program, often described simply as "University Collaborations".He is also a volunteer on IEEE's Humanitarian Activities Committee. Specifically, I am honored to have been appointed as 2018 Chair of the IEEE SIGHT Steering Committee. Kristen MacCartney

Senior University Partnership Program (UPP) Manager, IEEE

Kristen directly supports unique programs with students, librarians, and faculty at 18 schools in the US, India, and China. She focuses on universities in UPP: the Indian Institute of Science, Indian Institute of Technology (Kharagpur), Peking University, Tsinghua University, Stanford, Berkeley, Caltech, MIT, Columbia, Dartmouth, UCSD, UCLA, Michigan, Texas A&M, Georgia Tech, Virginia Tech, Drexel and WPI; but embraces outreach to any student/groups interested in learning more about the IEEE. She partners with the IEEE Client Services team and others throughout the Institute to reach engineers and technologists around the world, raising awareness of the information and opportunities provided by the IEEE that lead to better research, leadership, teamwork, networking, and communication skills. Kristen joined the IEEE in 2005 from Rutgers University, where she networked her way back into the workforce while completing her MLIS.   She also has an MBA from Rutgers and an English degree from Lafayette College; these degrees opened doors to an early career in financial writing (business plans, annual reports, and corporate PR.) She serves in leadership roles with various library and community organizations in Ohio and New Jersey.
